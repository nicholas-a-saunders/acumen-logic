Welcome & Course Overview

Your complete roadmap to passing UK finance aptitude tests — from Big 4 to investment banking.

1

Who This Course Is For

This course is built for graduates and career-changers preparing for the online aptitude screening stage at:

Big 4 Accounting Firms — EY, PwC, Deloitte, KPMG

Investment Banks — Goldman Sachs, JP Morgan, Barclays, HSBC, Citi, UBS, Rothschild, Macquarie, Deutsche Bank, Jefferies

Consulting & Financial Services — McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Schroders, Standard Chartered, BNP Paribas

Scope

This course covers the initial online screening test — the first hurdle in the application process. It does not cover interviews, assessment centres, or case studies. Pass the aptitude test first; everything else follows.

2

Your Learning Pathway

Six modules take you from foundations through to full mock tests under exam conditions.

3

How This Course Teaches

Every lesson follows the same method. We never start with theory — we always start with a real example.

See the question → Watch the method → Understand the principle → Practise under pressure

You'll see the exact question format used in real SHL, Aon, and Korn Ferry tests. Every worked example shows each calculation step individually — nothing is skipped or compressed.

How to study

For every worked example: attempt the question yourself first, then check the solution. Reviewing mistakes is where the real learning happens — don't just check whether you got it right.

Recommended Study Schedules

4–6 Week Plan

Week Focus
Week 1 M1 M2 Foundations + Numerical Core Skills
Week 2 M3 Data Interpretation & Applied Numeracy
Week 3 M4 Verbal & Logical Reasoning
Week 4 M5 Situational Judgement & Behavioural
Weeks 5–6 M6 Mock Tests, Exam Strategy & Revision

2 Week Crash Course

Week Focus
Week 1 M1 M2 M3 Foundations + All Numerical Content
Week 2 M4 M6 Verbal/Logical + Mock Tests & Strategy
Short on time?

If you have less than two weeks, prioritise Modules 2, 3, and 6. Numerical reasoning appears in virtually every UK finance screening test and is the most trainable skill. The mock tests in Module 6 build exam stamina you can't get from content alone.

Key Takeaways

  • This course covers the 5 test types used in UK finance screening: numerical, verbal, logical, SJT, and behavioural.
  • Numerical reasoning is the most common and most trainable skill — it gets two full modules.
  • Every lesson is example-led. You see the question before you see the theory.
  • Always attempt worked examples yourself before reading the solution.
  • Adapt your study schedule to your timeline: 4–6 weeks for thorough prep, 2 weeks for a focused crash course.
