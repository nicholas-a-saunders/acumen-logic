Essential Maths Refresh

Sharpen the fundamental tools. Not remedial maths — speed and accuracy under pressure.

1

BIDMAS / Order of Operations

BIDMAS dictates the order in which operations are performed. Getting this wrong doesn't just lose you one mark — it cascades through every multi-step calculation.

Brackets → Indices (powers) → Division → Multiplication → Addition → Subtraction

Division and multiplication are equal priority (work left to right). Addition and subtraction are equal priority (work left to right).

Worked Example What is 8 + 4 × 3 − 2 ?
✗ Wrong Approach
8 + 4 = 12
12 × 3 = 36
36 − 2 = 34
34 ✗
✓ Correct (BIDMAS)
4 × 3 = 12 (multiply first)
8 + 12 = 20
20 − 2 = 18
18 ✓
Common Trap What is 24 ÷ 6 × 2 ?
✗ Wrong
6 × 2 = 12
24 ÷ 12 = 2
2 ✗
✓ Correct (left to right)
24 ÷ 6 = 4 (÷ and × same priority)
4 × 2 = 8
8 ✓

When operations have equal priority, always work left to right.

2

Negative Numbers

These rules appear constantly in profit/loss, temperature, and percentage decrease questions.

positive × positive= positive
negative × negative= positive
positive × negative= negative
adding a negative= subtracting

These same rules apply to division. The sign rules for ÷ are identical to ×.

Finance Context A company's quarterly profits: Q1 = −£45k, Q2 = −£12k, Q3 = +£28k, Q4 = +£65k. Total annual profit?
1(−45) + (−12) = −57
228 + 65 = 93
393 + (−57) = 93 − 57 = 36
Annual profit: +£36,000
3

Multiplication Shortcuts

These save 5–15 seconds per question in timed tests. In an 18-question SHL test, that's potentially 2–3 extra minutes for harder questions.

× 5
Multiply by 10, then divide by 2
48 × 5 → 480 ÷ 2 = 240
× 25
Multiply by 100, then divide by 4
36 × 25 → 3,600 ÷ 4 = 900
× 11
Multiply by 10, then add the original
73 × 11 → 730 + 73 = 803
× 9
Multiply by 10, then subtract the original
56 × 9 → 560 − 56 = 504
Double / Half
Make one number easier: halve one, double the other
35 × 24 → 70 × 12 = 840
4

Division Shortcuts & Divisibility

÷ 5
Multiply by 2, then divide by 10
365 ÷ 5 → 730 ÷ 10 = 73
Factor
Break large divisions into smaller steps
480 ÷ 12 → 480 ÷ 4 = 120, then 120 ÷ 3 = 40

Quick divisibility checks:

÷ 2 — last digit is even. ÷ 3 — digit sum divisible by 3 (e.g., 147: 1+4+7 = 12 ✓). ÷ 4 — last two digits divisible by 4. ÷ 5 — ends in 0 or 5. ÷ 9 — digit sum divisible by 9. ÷ 10 — ends in 0.

5

Estimation & Rounding

Why Estimation Matters £847,320 × 38.7% = ?

Before reaching for a calculator, estimate: £850,000 × 40% = £340,000. If your calculated answer is £327,793 — it checks out. If it's £32,779 — you've misplaced a decimal point.

Estimation catches errors that cost marks. It also helps eliminate wrong options in multiple-choice questions before calculating precisely.

Rounding Strategy

When estimating, round each number to 1 significant figure: £847,320 ≈ £850,000 and 38.7% ≈ 40%. This takes 3 seconds and gives you a sanity check for every calculation.

Key Takeaways

  • BIDMAS errors cascade — get the order of operations right before anything else.
  • Equal-priority operations (÷ and ×, + and −) are always resolved left to right.
  • Use multiplication shortcuts (×5, ×9, ×11, ×25, double/half) to save 5–15 seconds per question.
  • Estimate before calculating — round to 1 significant figure for a 3-second sanity check.
  • These aren't "basic" skills. They're the foundation that makes everything in Modules 2–3 faster.
