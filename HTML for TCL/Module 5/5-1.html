Time Management & Test Strategy

The techniques that separate candidates who finish confidently from those who run out of time. Immediately actionable in every practice session and real test.

1

Time Budgets by Provider

Every provider allocates a different amount of time per question. Knowing your time budget before you start lets you make split-second decisions about when to skip, guess, or grind through a tough problem.

2

The 3-Pass Method

The most effective time management strategy for aptitude tests. Instead of working through questions sequentially, you make three deliberate passes through the test.

Pass 1 — Quick Wins (60% of time)
Answer every question you can solve in under your time budget
If you're stuck after 30 seconds of reading, flag it and move on immediately.
Pass 2 — Medium Difficulty (30% of time)
Return to flagged questions that need more thought
You now have the confidence of banked answers and a clearer head.
Pass 3 — Educated Guesses (10% of time)
For remaining questions, eliminate options and make your best guess
Never leave a question blank. Most tests have no penalty for wrong answers.
The #1 Time Killer

Spending 4 minutes on a single hard question while 3 easy questions remain unanswered. Every easy question is worth the same as a hard one. Maximise correct answers, not difficulty conquered.

3

Educated Guessing

When time runs out, random guessing gives you a 25% chance on 4-option MCQs. Educated guessing can push that to 50% or higher. Here's how:

Elimination by magnitude: If a numerical question asks for a percentage and your options are 8%, 15%, 23%, 47% — a quick estimate can often rule out the extremes. If you know the answer is "in the teens," you're already 50/50 between 8% and 15%.

Trap answer elimination: One answer is always the result of a common mistake (e.g., dividing by the wrong base, adding percentages instead of multiplying). If you can identify it, eliminate it.

Verbal reasoning default: When truly stuck on T/F/CS, "Cannot Say" is the statistically safest guess — it's correct more often than candidates expect, because test designers include it to punish confident over-interpreters.

No Negative Marking

SHL, Korn Ferry, and Cappfinity tests do not penalise wrong answers. There is never a reason to leave a question blank. Aon adaptive tests are the exception — wrong answers may reduce your difficulty level — but leaving questions blank is still worse.

4

Calculator Discipline

Most numerical tests allow an on-screen calculator or your own physical calculator. The biggest mistake is using it for everything — or not using it when you should.

Use Mental MathsUse the Calculator
10%, 20%, 25%, 50% of a numberAnything involving 3+ digit division
Simple addition/subtractionCompound growth (1.08³)
Doubling, halving, multiplying by 5Multi-step percentage changes
Comparing obvious magnitudesWeighted averages with large numbers
Calculator Trap

Typing long numbers incorrectly. Always estimate the answer before using the calculator: "This should be roughly £2 million." If your calculator shows £200,000 or £20 million, you've made a data entry error. The 2-second sanity check saves the 30-second recalculation.

5

The 5-Minute Pre-Test Routine

Before the timer starts, execute this checklist:

1. Know your time budget. Total time ÷ total questions = seconds per question. Write it down.

2. Calculator ready. Physical calculator out, on, with batteries checked. Or confirm the on-screen calculator works.

3. Scratch paper. Pen and paper at hand for working out (if online test allows it).

4. Environment. Quiet room, good internet connection, browser in full-screen mode, all notifications off.

5. Mental reset. Three slow breaths. Remind yourself: "Easy questions first. Skip hard ones. Come back."

Key Takeaways

  • Know your time budget for every provider format — SHL verbal (38s) demands a different approach than SHL numerical (83s).
  • Use the 3-pass method: quick wins first, medium questions second, educated guesses last. Never stall on one question.
  • Never leave blanks. Most tests have no negative marking. A 25% guess is infinitely better than 0%.
  • Estimate before calculating. The 2-second sanity check catches data entry errors that cost 30+ seconds.
  • Your pre-test routine is as important as your knowledge. Environment, equipment, and mindset compound into performance.
