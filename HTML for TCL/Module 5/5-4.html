Personality Tests & Gamified Assessments

Big Five traits, Arctic Shores, and Pymetrics — assessments you can't fake, and honest guidance on how to approach them authentically.

1

The Big Five Personality Model

Most personality assessments in finance are based on the Big Five (OCEAN) model. Understanding what's being measured helps you approach the test calmly — not to fake it, but to avoid anxiety about "right" answers.

TraitWhat It MeasuresFinance Preference
OpennessCuriosity, creativity, openness to new ideasModerate — innovative but grounded
ConscientiousnessOrganisation, reliability, attention to detailHigh — finance demands precision
ExtraversionSociability, assertiveness, energyVaries by role — higher for client-facing
AgreeablenessCooperation, trust, empathyModerate — collaborative but able to challenge
NeuroticismEmotional stability, stress responseLow — calm under pressure is essential
Do Not Try to Fake

Modern personality tests have built-in consistency checks. They ask the same question in different ways at different points in the test. Contradictory answers flag your profile as inconsistent — which is worse than any "unfavourable" trait score.

2

Gamified Assessments: Arctic Shores & Pymetrics

Arctic Shores uses game-based tasks to measure cognitive traits: risk tolerance, processing speed, working memory, and decision-making under uncertainty. The "games" include things like balloon-inflating (risk appetite), card-matching (working memory), and reaction tasks (processing speed).

Pymetrics uses neuroscience-based games to assess 90+ cognitive and emotional traits. Each game takes 2–3 minutes. Tasks include the balloon game, delayed gratification tasks, and emotion recognition exercises.

What They Actually Measure

These aren't IQ tests. They measure behavioural tendencies — how you approach risk, how quickly you adapt to changing rules, whether you explore or exploit. There's no single "correct" profile. Different roles at the same employer may have different target profiles.

3

Honest Preparation Strategies

You can't study for personality or gamified assessments, but you can prepare your conditions:

1. Take them when rested. Fatigue increases neuroticism scores and slows processing speed. Do them in the morning, not at midnight.

2. Minimise distractions. These tests measure reaction times and decision speed. A noisy environment introduces noise into your data.

3. Be consistent. Answer the way you genuinely feel, not the way you think they want. The consistency checks will catch you.

4. Don't overthink. Personality questions have no right answer. Speed of response matters — hesitation suggests you're calculating rather than responding naturally.

5. Accept the outcome. If your personality doesn't match a role's target profile, that role genuinely isn't a good fit. Better to find out now than six months into a miserable job.

The "Desirability" Trap

Some candidates answer every question with the most "positive" option: "I always lead teams," "I never feel stressed," "I always meet deadlines." This creates an impossibly perfect profile that the test flags as socially desirable responding. Real humans have imperfections — your profile should too.

4

When Gamified Assessments Feel Unfair

Many candidates feel frustrated by gamified assessments because there's no clear way to "prepare." This is by design — they measure natural cognitive tendencies, not learned skills.

The good news: if you pass the aptitude tests (Modules 2–4), your cognitive abilities are likely strong enough for the gamified assessments. These tests primarily screen for extremes — very high risk-taking, very low processing speed, or very poor emotional regulation — not minor trait differences.

Focus your preparation energy on the aptitude and SJT components, where practice genuinely improves performance. For personality and gamified assessments, prepare your environment and mindset, then trust your natural responses.

Key Takeaways

  • Personality tests measure traits, not skills. They use the Big Five model. Finance values conscientiousness and emotional stability most.
  • Do not fake. Consistency checks will catch you. An inconsistent profile is worse than any individual trait score.
  • Gamified assessments measure behavioural tendencies — how you approach risk, adapt to change, and process information. There's no "studying" for them.
  • Prepare your conditions: rested, quiet environment, morning timing. These factors genuinely affect your results.
  • Trust your natural responses. If your personality doesn't match the role, that's useful information — not a failure.
