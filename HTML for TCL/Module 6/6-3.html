Module 6 · Lesson 6.3

Mock 1 Debrief & Analysis

Your score is just a number. What matters is understanding WHY you got questions wrong — and fixing the pattern before Mock 2.

1

The 5-Step Debrief Framework

Open your Mock 1 results and work through these five steps. Write your answers down — the act of writing forces deeper analysis than just reading.

Step 1 — Score Snapshot
Record: Correct / Total / Percentage / Time taken
If you timed yourself: did you finish with time left, or rush the last few questions?
Step 2 — Error Categorisation
For each wrong answer: Speed Error | Reverse Base | Time Spiral | Over-Reading | Anchor Bias
Refer to Lesson 5.5 for the five error pattern definitions.
Step 3 — Topic Analysis
Count errors by topic: Percentages | Ratios | Fractions | Averages | Data | Margins
Which topic produced the most errors? That's your priority revision area.
Step 4 — Trap Analysis
For each wrong answer: was the trap answer one of the distractors you selected?
If yes, you're falling for designed traps. Review the specific trap type (reverse %, wrong base, etc.).
Step 5 — One Focus Area
Choose ONE specific weakness to fix before Mock 2
Not two. Not three. One thing, practised until it's automatic.

Revision Priorities by Topic

Based on your error analysis, revisit these lessons:

Percentages: Lesson 2.1 (especially reverse percentages and the "wrong base" trap)

Ratios: Lesson 2.2 (ratio changes and proportion scaling)

Fractions: Lesson 2.3 (LCD method for remainder questions)

Averages/Growth: Lesson 2.4 (weighted averages and compound change — the most commonly failed topic)

Data Interpretation: Lessons 3.1–3.3 (table reading, chart interpretation, multi-source integration)

Financial Statements: Lesson 3.5 (profit margins and break-even)

4

Before Mock 2

Your Plan

1. Complete the debrief above. 2. Revisit your weakest topic lesson. 3. Re-do the practice questions from that lesson. 4. Wait at least 24 hours before attempting Mock 2 — sleep consolidates learning. 5. Mock 2 includes verbal and logical reasoning alongside numerical — review Lessons 4.1–4.4 if needed.

Key Principles

  • Scores improve when you fix patterns, not when you practise more random questions.
  • One focused revision session on your weakest topic is worth more than re-doing the entire mock.
  • Sleep between sessions. Your brain consolidates procedural knowledge during sleep — don't cram Mock 2 right after debrief.
  • Mock 2 is mixed format. You'll need verbal and logical skills too. Allocate revision time accordingly.
