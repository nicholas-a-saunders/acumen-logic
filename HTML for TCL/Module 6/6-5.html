Test Day Checklist & Final Tips

You've done the preparation. Now make sure nothing practical goes wrong on the day. This is your pre-flight checklist.

1

The Night Before

  • Check your invitation email. Confirm the test provider (SHL, Aon, Korn Ferry, etc.), time window, and link. Some tests expire — don't leave it to the last minute.
  • Test your equipment. Use the same computer you'll take the test on. Check your internet connection, webcam (if required), and that your browser is up to date. SHL recommends Chrome or Firefox.
  • Close unnecessary applications. Notifications, auto-updates, and background downloads can slow your machine or distract you mid-test.
  • Prepare your workspace. Clear desk. Good lighting. Quiet room. Have a glass of water, a pen, and scrap paper ready.
  • Charge your calculator. If using a physical calculator, check the batteries. If using the on-screen calculator, practise the keyboard shortcuts (most providers support numpad input).
  • Set two alarms. One for 30 minutes before you plan to start, one for 15 minutes before. Give yourself a buffer.
  • Sleep. 7+ hours. No cramming. Your brain consolidates learning during sleep — last-minute study does more harm than good.
2

The Morning Of

  • Eat a proper breakfast. Your brain needs glucose. Avoid heavy meals that make you sluggish — aim for slow-release energy (porridge, eggs, wholemeal toast).
  • Light physical activity. A 10-minute walk or light stretching reduces cortisol and improves focus. Don't exhaust yourself.
  • Review — don't cram. Spend 5–10 minutes glancing at your formula reference sheet (Lesson 6.1). Do NOT try to learn new material.
  • Log in early. Open the test link 10–15 minutes before your scheduled time. Complete any identity verification or system checks early.
3

During the Test: The 3-Pass Reminder

Pass 1 — Quick Wins (First 40% of Time)
Answer every question you can solve in under 90 seconds
Flag anything that needs more time and move on. Don't get stuck.
Pass 2 — Considered (Next 40% of Time)
Return to flagged questions and work through them methodically
These are questions you can solve but need more thought or calculation.
Pass 3 — Educated Guesses (Final 20% of Time)
For any remaining unanswered questions: eliminate 1–2 options, then guess
Never leave a question blank. Most tests have no penalty for wrong answers.
4

Emergency Protocols

ProblemWhat To Do
Internet dropsMost platforms auto-save your progress. Reconnect immediately. If the timer kept running, contact the employer's recruitment team and explain — they can usually reset the test.
Computer crashesSame as above. Your answers are typically saved server-side. Restart, reconnect, and continue. Contact recruitment if time was lost.
Can't read a chartTry zooming in (Ctrl/Cmd + scroll). If the image is truly broken, take a screenshot and email recruitment — don't waste 5 minutes staring at it.
Time running outImmediately answer all remaining questions with your best educated guess. A guessed answer has a 25% chance; a blank answer has a 0% chance.
Panic / mind goes blank4-7-8 breathing: inhale 4 seconds, hold 7, exhale 8. Then re-read the question slowly. Focus on what you know, not what you don't.
5

Common Test-Day Mistakes

Starting before you're ready. Use the practice/sample questions at the beginning of the test to calibrate. Don't rush through them — they set your rhythm.

Spending 4+ minutes on one question. If you can't solve it in 2 minutes, flag and move on. Return later. The time you waste on a hard question could answer two easier ones.

Not reading the question fully. "What is the percentage increase?" vs "What is the new value after the increase?" — these require different calculations. Read every word.

Changing answers without reason. Your first instinct is statistically more likely to be correct. Only change an answer if you spot a clear calculation error.

Forgetting to submit. Some platforms auto-submit when time expires. Others don't. Know which type you're using and click "Submit" if required.

6

After the Test

Don't over-analyse. You won't remember every question accurately. Your perception of how it went is often wrong — wait for the actual result.

Note what surprised you. If you encounter question types or topics you didn't expect, write them down. This helps if you need to retake or prepare for a different employer's test.

Prepare for the next stage. Many firms follow aptitude tests with video interviews or assessment centres. Start preparing early — don't wait for results.

  • Sleep, eat, breathe. Your brain is your instrument. Treat it well on test day.
