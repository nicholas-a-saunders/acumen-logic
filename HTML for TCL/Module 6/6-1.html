Module 6 · Lesson 6.1

Pre-Assessment Strategy Review

Everything you've learned, distilled into a rapid-reference checklist. Review this before every mock and every real test.

1

Numerical Reasoning — Key Rules

Percentages
% change = (New − Old) ÷ Old × 100 · Reverse %: divide by (1 ± rate)
Always divide by the ORIGINAL value. The trap answer always uses the new value.
Ratios
Total parts = sum of ratio · Each share = (its parts ÷ total parts) × total amount
Averages & Growth
Weighted average = Sum(value × weight) ÷ Sum(weights) · Never average averages
Sequential % changes multiply (× 1.10 × 0.95), they don't add (+10% − 5%).
CAGR
(End ÷ Start)^(1/n) − 1 · In tests: work backwards from answer options
2

Data Interpretation — Key Rules

Read the question first, then the data. Don't waste time understanding every number in a table when you only need two cells.

Check units. Tables mix £millions, £thousands, and raw numbers. Misreading the unit header is the #1 data interpretation error.

Multi-source questions: Extract all needed numbers onto scratch paper before calculating. Don't toggle between tables and charts.

Stacked bar charts: Each segment's value = its top edge minus its bottom edge, not the position of its top edge.

3

Logical Reasoning — Key Rules

Deductive: Use the contrapositive. "If A then B" = "If not B then not A." This unlocks most deductive questions.

Verbal (T/F/CS): Read the statement first, then scan the passage. TRUE = directly supported. FALSE = directly contradicted. CANNOT SAY = neither supported nor contradicted, even if you know the answer from outside knowledge.

Rule application: Follow conditional chains — one rule triggers another. Check for contradictions. Use elimination on the answer options.

Three Verbal Traps to Remember

1. Outside knowledge: You know the answer from general knowledge, but the passage doesn't state it → Cannot Say. 2. Probability: "Company X is likely to..." → Cannot Say unless the passage explicitly states likelihood. 3. Qualifier swap: Passage says "some" but statement says "all" → False.

4

Test Strategy — The Checklist

Before the test: Know your time budget. Calculator ready. Scratch paper at hand. Quiet environment. Three slow breaths.

During the test: 3-pass method — quick wins, medium difficulty, educated guesses. Never spend more than 2 minutes on one question. Never leave blanks (no negative marking on most tests).

When stuck: Eliminate at least one option, then guess from the remainder. A 33% guess beats a blank.

After the test: Categorise errors, identify your dominant pattern, focus your next practice session on that specific weakness.

5

Mental Readiness

Your Mindset Going In

You've completed 30 lessons of targeted preparation. You understand percentages, ratios, data interpretation, logical reasoning, verbal reasoning, and SJTs at a level that most candidates never reach. The test is designed to be challenging — difficulty is normal. Trust your training, execute your method, and manage your time.

Remind yourself: "Easy questions first. Skip hard ones. Come back. Never leave blanks. Estimate before calculating. Check direction."

This simple mantra, internalised, is worth more than any last-minute cramming.
