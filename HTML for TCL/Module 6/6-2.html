Percentage Change
A company's quarterly revenue fell from £8.4 million to £7.14 million. What was the percentage decrease?
A
12%
B
14%
C
15%
D
17.6%

(£8.4m − £7.14m) ÷ £8.4m = £1.26m ÷ £8.4m = 15%. Option D (17.6%) is the trap — dividing by the new value instead of the original.

2
Reverse Percentage
After a 12% price increase, a subscription costs £134.40. What was the original price?
A
£118.27
B
£120.00
C
£122.40
D
£150.53

£134.40 ÷ 1.12 = £120.00. Option A is £134.40 × 0.88 — subtracting 12% from the new price (wrong method). Option D is £134.40 × 1.12 — going in the wrong direction.

3
Ratio
A fund allocates assets in the ratio 7 : 4 : 1 between equities, bonds, and cash. If the fund is worth £3.6 million, how much is in bonds?
A
£1,200,000
B
£1,050,000
C
£300,000
D
£2,100,000

Total parts = 7 + 4 + 1 = 12. Bonds = 4/12 × £3.6m = £1,200,000. Option D (£2.1m) is equities (7 parts). Option C (£300k) is cash.

Weighted Average
A bank has two loan portfolios. Portfolio A: £50m at 4.2% interest. Portfolio B: £30m at 6.8% interest. What is the weighted average interest rate across both portfolios?
A
5.10%
B
5.25%
C
5.50%
D
5.18%

(50 × 4.2 + 30 × 6.8) ÷ (50 + 30) = (210 + 204) ÷ 80 = 414 ÷ 80 = 5.175% ≈ 5.18%. Option C (5.50%) is the simple average — the classic trap.

Compound Change
A share price rises 15% in Year 1, falls 8% in Year 2, and rises 10% in Year 3. What is the overall percentage change?
A
+15.6%
B
+16.4%
C
+17.0%
D
+17.3%

1.15 × 0.92 × 1.10 = 1.1638. Overall change = +16.38% ≈ +16.4%. Adding the percentages (15 − 8 + 10 = 17%) gives C — the trap answer.

Fractions
A project budget of £840,000 is allocated: ⅓ to development, ¼ to marketing, ⅛ to legal. How much remains unallocated?
A
£210,000
B
£235,000
C
£245,000
D
£280,000

LCD = 24. ⅓ = 8/24, ¼ = 6/24, ⅛ = 3/24. Sum = 17/24. Remainder = 7/24. £840,000 × 7/24 = £840,000 ÷ 24 × 7 = £35,000 × 7 = £245,000.

Currency Conversion
A UK company receives €234,000 from a European client. The exchange rate is £1 = €1.17. What is the GBP equivalent?
A
£200,000
B
£213,600
C
£273,780
D
£190,000

€234,000 ÷ 1.17 = £200,000. Option C (£273,780) is €234,000 × 1.17 — multiplying instead of dividing (wrong direction). Sanity check: £ should be less than €.

Data Interpretation
A retail company's quarterly sales (£ millions): Q1: 12.4, Q2: 14.8, Q3: 13.2, Q4: 16.6
What percentage of total annual sales occurred in Q4?
A
25.0%
B
28.4%
C
29.1%
D
31.2%

Total = 12.4 + 14.8 + 13.2 + 16.6 = 57.0. Q4 as % = 16.6 ÷ 57.0 × 100 = 29.12% ≈ 29.1%.

9
Profit Margin
Revenue: £2.4m. Cost of goods: £1.56m. Operating expenses: £420,000. What is the operating profit margin?
A
15.0%
B
17.5%
C
21.2%
D
35.0%

Operating profit = Revenue − COGS − OpEx = £2.4m − £1.56m − £0.42m = £0.42m. Margin = £0.42m ÷ £2.4m × 100 = 17.5%. Option D (35%) is the gross margin (£2.4m − £1.56m = £0.84m → 35%).

10
CAGR
An investment grew from £25,000 to £33,275.31 over 4 years. What is the CAGR? (Work backwards from the options.)
A
6%
B
7%
C
7.5%
D
7.4%

Try 7%: £25,000 × 1.07⁴ = £25,000 × 1.3108 = £32,770 — too low. Try 7.5%: £25,000 × 1.075⁴ = £25,000 × 1.3355 = £33,388 — too high. Answer is between 7% and 7.5%. Try 7.4%: 1.074⁴ ≈ 1.331 → £25,000 × 1.331 = £33,275. Answer: D (7.4%).

11
Depreciation
Equipment worth £96,000 depreciates at 20% per year (compound). What is the book value after 3 years?
A
£49,152
B
£38,400
C
£57,600
D
£46,080

£96,000 × 0.80³ = £96,000 × 0.512 = £49,152. Option B (£38,400) is straight-line (£96k − 3 × 20% × £96k = £96k − £57.6k = £38.4k). Compound always leaves a higher value.

12
Multi-Step
Company A's revenue is £5.2m with a 22% operating margin. Company B's revenue is £3.8m with a 28% operating margin. What is the combined operating profit?
A
£2,150,000
B
£2,180,000
C
£2,208,000
D
£2,250,000

A profit = £5.2m × 0.22 = £1,144,000. B profit = £3.8m × 0.28 = £1,064,000. Combined = £2,208,000. Trap: averaging the margins (25%) × total revenue (£9m) = £2.25m (option D) — the "average of averages" fallacy.

13
Percentage Of
A department employs 240 staff. 65% are full-time. Of the full-time staff, 40% have management responsibilities. How many full-time managers are there?
A
56
B
62
C
96
D
156

Full-time = 240 × 0.65 = 156. Managers = 156 × 0.40 = 62.4 → 62. Option C (96) is 240 × 0.40 — applying 40% to total staff, not just full-time. Option D (156) is just the full-time count.

14
Index Numbers
An index starts at 100 in 2020. It reaches 118 in 2022 and 127 in 2024. What was the percentage increase from 2022 to 2024?
A
7.6%
B
9.0%
C
27.0%
D
7.1%

(127 − 118) ÷ 118 × 100 = 9 ÷ 118 = 7.63% ≈ 7.6%. Option B (9%) confuses the index point change (9 points) with the percentage change. Option C (27%) uses the base year 100 instead of 2022's value.

15
Ratio Change
In 2023, a company's debt-to-equity ratio was 3:5. In 2024, debt increased by 20% while equity remained unchanged. What is the new ratio in simplest form?
A
4 : 5
B
3 : 4
C
7 : 10
D
18 : 25

Original debt : equity = 3 : 5. Debt increases 20%: 3 × 1.2 = 3.6. New ratio = 3.6 : 5. Multiply by 5 to clear decimals: 18 : 25. Answer: 18 : 25.

16
Break-Even
Fixed costs: £180,000. Variable cost per unit: £12. Selling price per unit: £30. How many units must be sold to break even?
A
6,000
B
10,000
C
15,000
D
9,000

Contribution per unit = £30 − £12 = £18. Break-even = £180,000 ÷ £18 = 10,000 units. Option A (6,000) divides by £30 (full selling price, ignoring variable costs).

Weighted Average
Customer satisfaction scores: Branch A (400 customers): 78%. Branch B (250 customers): 85%. Branch C (150 customers): 92%.
What is the company-wide weighted average satisfaction score?
A
83.1%
B
84.0%
C
82.6%
D
85.0%

(400×78 + 250×85 + 150×92) ÷ (400+250+150) = (31,200 + 21,250 + 13,800) ÷ 800 = 66,250 ÷ 800 = 82.8125 ≈ 82.6%. Option D (85%) is the simple average ((78+85+92)÷3) — the trap.

Multi-Source
Total employees: 1,200. Technology department: 18% of total. Average salary across company: £42,000. Technology average salary: £56,000.
What is the total annual salary cost for the Technology department?
A
£12,096,000
B
£9,072,000
C
£50,400,000

£10,080,000

Tech staff = 1,200 × 0.18 = 216. Total salary = 216 × £56,000 = £12,096,000. Option B uses the company average salary (216 × £42,000). Option C uses total employees × tech salary (1,200 × £42,000).
